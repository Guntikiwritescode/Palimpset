Dear Six Degrees of Francis Bacon team,

I'm building a non-commercial, portfolio research project that models early-modern
social networks as an uncertainty-aware knowledge graph, and I'd like to use the
downloadable SDFB relationship/person data as one source, with full attribution.

A 2015 post on the project's blog stated that SDFB shares its data free of charge
for other researchers' **non-commercial** purposes, without warranty, asking at a
minimum that the project's website be cited. I'd be grateful if you could confirm,
in a sentence or two, that **this still holds** for the current data export — i.e.
that non-commercial reuse with attribution (citing sixdegreesoffrancisbacon.com) is
permitted.

To be concrete about my use:
- non-commercial only; the data is not redistributed — I ingest it into a private
  system and display derived views with a link back to your project;
- every relationship/date I render is marked as *derived* and attributed to SDFB,
  and I state plainly that your confidence scores are a bootstrap selection
  frequency, not a probability;
- until I have your written confirmation, the project is **not** publicly deployed
  and its data is not published (this is enforced in the system, not just intended).

A one-line "yes, that still applies" is all I need to lift that gate. If there is a
specific licence or citation form you'd prefer, please point me to it.

Thank you for building and sharing SDFB — it's a remarkable resource.

Best regards,
[your name]
